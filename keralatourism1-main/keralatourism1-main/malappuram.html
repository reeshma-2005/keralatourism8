MALAPPURAM



Malappuram is a district of the Kerala state with lots of point of interest for the tourist. The whole district contains the many of the places in which national parks, rivers, lake, waterfall, temples and lighthouses are the main. There are so many temples in this place to attract the people for showing the culture and tradition of Kerala. There is a Silent valley national park, which including backpacking, rivers, forest, tiger reserves and rainforest. This national park mostly covers the area of river and forest. Remaining part of the park is used to grow the tree plantation for fresh environment. In the other hand, there is a waterfall and a light house.

Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary: A Treat To Eyes

A remarkable sanctuary hosting more than 100s of native bird species and 60 migratory bird species is widely known for its location.

Keralmkundu Waterfalls: Enticing Sights

It is another fresh experience to get yourself soaked in the pond of fresh Keralamkundu waterfalls .

Padinharekera Beach: Scenic Beauty

Padinharekara beach is one of the tourist spots that people want to visit most frequently. You can have a mesmerizing view of two rivers Bharathapuzha and Tirupuzha merging with Arabian sea. It is one of the best places to visit in Malappuram.

Kovilakam: Royal Residence Of Nilambers

The popular deity of the local community and the royal residence of Nilambers, the temple celebrates a six-day festival called Nilambar Pattu Ulsavam by singing hymns in praise of lord shiva

Bharathapuzha River: Second Longest River In Kerala

This is one of the few rivers in Kerala with a spiritual and cultural significance for people, especially the famous cultural house of the state Kalamandalam is situated on the banks of the river.
